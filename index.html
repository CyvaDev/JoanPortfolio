About Me

Hi! I’m Joan, a game designer passionate about Level Design and UI Design. I used to dream of being an architect, until I discovered Game Design and found a way to build worlds, tell stories, and create experiences all at once.

I love crafting levels that challenge and immerse players, and designing intuitive interfaces that make gameplay smooth and engaging.

Outside of games, I enjoy photography and playing guitar, two hobbies that keep my creativity alive and help me see the world from new angles.

I’m always open to new projects, collaborations, and ways to keep growing as a designer.

Bitsy Game - An unsolved Case.

"A detective trapped in a spiral of mystery and blood, where every clue brings him closer to the truth... and danger. Can you uncover the killer before it's too late?"

An Unsolved Case is a short 2D narrative game made in Bitsy, where you take on the role of a detective trying to solve a brutal murder.

Created by me as a personal experiment in minimalist storytelling and limited design tools.

The game was developed in just a few days using Bitsy, with a focus on atmosphere, tension, and narrative pacing through simple visuals and interactions.

TPS ARPG Game - No Oxygen Left!

No Oxygen Left! is a small third-person ARPG game developed over the course of three months.

It features mechanics inspired by games like Dead Space, where oxygen is limited and players must scavenge for capsules scattered throughout the world while fighting off enemies.

This project focuses on level blocking and the use of design techniques such as foreshadowing in specific rooms. Verticality was also a key goal, aiming to create a more dynamic and less flat environment.

The constant search for oxygen adds a continuous layer of tension, the core experience the game is built around..

Puzzle Script Game - Skiing on ICE

Skiing on ICE! is a simple yet challenging 2D puzzle game built with PuzzleScript, where every move counts!

Glide across slippery ice, carefully planning each slide to reach your goal without overshooting.

The game was developed in just 4 days as a creative exercise in constraint-based design, using PuzzleScript's minimalistic tools to focus on smart level design and satisfying mechanics.

Idle Game - Idle Icecube Farm

Idle Icecube Farm is a small video game developed in under three days, inspired by titles like Cookie Clicker.

IIF (Idle Icecube Farm) is an idle clicker with a playtime of around 20 minutes, where your goal is to produce as many ice cubes per second as possible.

Created using Construct 3 and developed solo.
